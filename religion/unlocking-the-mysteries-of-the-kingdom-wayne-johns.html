There are many conflicting voices today about the Kingdom of God.

Who’s right and who’s wrong? 

With astute precision, author Wayne Johns dissects the Scriptures to explore the implications of relating to a God who loves us passionately. Both affirming and suspicious viewpoints about Kingdom teaching are clearly discussed, and a balanced understanding emerges that charts the way forward between opposing sides.

Areas of controversy that are soundly navigated include the role of repentance, the Fatherhood of God, our place in the Kingdom, and the supernatural power of the Gospel.

Wayne points out that the Kingdom is like a house with repentance as the door. While repentance is a vital ingredient of the Christian life, there is a danger of camping at the door and never exploring the house. This can limit and distort our perception of God, ourselves and people outside of the Church.

This is an exciting and informative read for Christian leaders and all believers who desire to know God better and to grow in freedom, discernment and the realities of His Kingdom.
